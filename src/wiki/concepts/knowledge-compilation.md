## Summary

Knowledge compilation is the process of transforming raw information into structured, interconnected knowledge that compounds over time rather than requiring rediscovery on each query.

## Key Insight

The tedious part of maintaining a knowledge base is not the reading or thinking — it's the bookkeeping. Updating cross-references, keeping summaries current, noting contradictions, maintaining consistency across pages.

Humans abandon wikis because the maintenance burden grows faster than the value. LLMs don't get bored, don't forget to update a cross-reference, and can touch 15 files in one pass.
